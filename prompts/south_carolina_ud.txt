SOUTH CAROLINA LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — EJECTMENT PROCEEDINGS:
- Governing statutes: S.C. Code Ann. §§ 27-37-10 through 27-37-170 (Ejectment); § 27-40-710 (SCRLTA — grounds and notice)
- Court: Magistrate Court in the county where the rental property is located.
- Prerequisite: Proper written notice must have been served and the notice period expired before filing.
- Application: Landlord files Application for Ejectment (official form SCCA/732) at Magistrate Court. For nonpayment, also file Affidavit and Itemization of Accounts (SCCA/733).
- Court process: Magistrate issues a "Rule to Show Cause" served on the tenant.
- Tenant response deadline: TEN (10) days after service of the Rule to Show Cause to appear or file an answer (§ 27-37-20). Default if no appearance.
- Writ of Ejectment: Issued within FIVE (5) days of judgment (§ 27-37-100). Constable/sheriff gives tenant TWENTY-FOUR (24) HOURS to vacate voluntarily (§ 27-37-160).
- Appeal: Either party may appeal within FIVE (5) days. Tenant must post an appeal bond within 5 days to stay eviction.
- Self-help eviction illegal: § 27-40-660 — violation results in liability for greater of 3 months' rent or twice actual damages.
- Mandatory property disposal language (§ 27-40-710(D)) must appear in the underlying notice.

GROUNDS SELECTION — based on {{reason_for_eviction}}:
- "Nonpayment": underlying notice = 5-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Vacate per § 27-40-710(B); include § 27-40-710(D) property disposal language
- "Lease Violation": underlying notice = 14-Day Notice to Cure or Vacate per § 27-40-710(A); describe violation; include § 27-40-710(D)
- "End of Lease" or "No-Fault": underlying notice = 30-Day Notice to Vacate per § 27-40-770; include § 27-40-710(D)
