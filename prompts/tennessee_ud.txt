CRITICAL TENNESSEE-SPECIFIC RULE — TWO-TIER LEGAL SYSTEM:
Tennessee applies DIFFERENT pre-suit notice requirements depending on county population.

URLTA COUNTIES (population over 75,000) — T.C.A. § 66-28-505 governs notice:
Anderson, Blount, Bradley, Davidson, Greene, Hamilton, Knox, Madison, Maury, Montgomery, Rutherford, Sevier, Shelby, Sullivan, Sumner, Washington, Williamson, Wilson

NON-URLTA COUNTIES (all others) — T.C.A. § 66-7-109 governs notice.

TENNESSEE LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — DETAINER WARRANT PROCEEDINGS:
- Governing statutes: T.C.A. §§ 29-18-101 through 29-18-135 (Forcible Entry and Detainer)
- Court: General Sessions Court in the county where the rental property is located.
- Prerequisite: Appropriate pre-suit notice (14-day for nonpayment/violations, 30-day for month-to-month termination) must have been served and expired.
- Filing: Landlord files a Detainer Warrant (civil warrant) with the General Sessions Court clerk.
- Hearing: Scheduled within 6 to 21 days of filing (T.C.A. § 29-18-118).
- Service of the Detainer Warrant (T.C.A. § 29-18-115): Must be served at least SIX (6) days before the hearing date. Primary: personal service. Alternative if 3 attempts fail: post on door + first-class mail (ONLY supports possession — NOT money judgment).
- Writ of Possession: Available starting Day 11 after judgment through Day 90. Sheriff executes.
- Appeal: Tenant has TEN (10) days after judgment to appeal to Circuit Court. Filing appeal does not stay execution — tenant must also file appeal bond.
- Self-help eviction illegal: T.C.A. § 66-28-518.

GROUNDS SELECTION — based on {{reason_for_eviction}} AND {{county}} URLTA/non-URLTA status:
- "Nonpayment" + URLTA county: 14-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Vacate per T.C.A. § 66-28-505(a)
- "Nonpayment" + Non-URLTA county: 14-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Vacate per T.C.A. § 66-7-109(a)(1)(A)
- "Lease Violation" + URLTA county: 14-Day Notice to Cure or Vacate per T.C.A. § 66-28-505(a)(1)-(2)

- "End of Lease" / "No-Fault": 30-Day Notice to Vacate per T.C.A. § 66-28-512 (URLTA) or 30-day common law (non-URLTA)
