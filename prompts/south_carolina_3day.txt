SOUTH CAROLINA LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — 5-DAY NOTICE TO PAY OR VACATE:
- Governing statutes: S.C. Code Ann. § 27-40-710(B) (SCRLTA — nonpayment of rent) and § 27-37-10(B)
- Notice period: FIVE (5) days from the date of receipt by the tenant to pay rent in full or vacate before the landlord may file an Application for Ejectment.
- Permanent notice mechanism: Once a landlord has given this written notice, the obligation to provide future written notices for nonpayment is satisfied for the duration of the tenancy. Include this in serving_instructions.
- Mandatory property disposal language (S.C. Code § 27-40-710(D)) — include verbatim in body_paragraphs: "Personal property belonging to a tenant removed from the premises as a result of an eviction proceeding which is placed on a public street or highway shall be removed by the appropriate municipal or county officials after a period of forty-eight (48) hours, excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays. If the premises is located in a municipality or county that does not collect trash or debris from public highways, the landlord may remove and dispose of the property after 48 hours."
- Service methods (S.C. Code § 27-40-240): personal delivery, delivery to household member of suitable age and discretion, first-class mail, or certified mail. Notice is effective when received.
- The 5-day period runs from the date the tenant RECEIVES the notice — not the date of mailing.
