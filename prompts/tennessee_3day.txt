CRITICAL TENNESSEE-SPECIFIC RULE — TWO-TIER LEGAL SYSTEM:
Tennessee has TWO separate bodies of eviction law depending on county population. You must apply the correct statute based on {{county}}.

URLTA COUNTIES (population over 75,000) — governed by T.C.A. § 66-28-505:
Anderson, Blount, Bradley, Davidson, Greene, Hamilton, Knox, Madison, Maury, Montgomery, Rutherford, Sevier, Shelby, Sullivan, Sumner, Washington, Williamson, Wilson

NON-URLTA COUNTIES (population under 75,000) — governed by T.C.A. § 66-7-109:
All counties NOT listed above.

TENNESSEE LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — NOTICE TO PAY RENT OR VACATE:
- Notice period: FOURTEEN (14) DAYS — applies under both URLTA (§ 66-28-505(a)) and non-URLTA (§ 66-7-109(a)(1)(A)).
- Grace period: Tennessee provides a FIVE (5)-day grace period (excluding legal holidays) after the rent due date. The 14-day notice may only be issued AFTER the grace period expires.
- First offense (tenant NOT late within prior 6 months): "14-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Vacate." Tenant has 14 days from receipt to pay or vacate.
- Repeat offense (tenant was late within prior 6 months AND previously received a 14-day notice):
    URLTA county: "7-Day Notice to Vacate" per T.C.A. § 66-28-505(a)(2)(B) — no cure option.
    Non-URLTA county: still 14-day notice under § 66-7-109(a)(1)(A).
- Default: if client data does not clarify prior delinquency, generate first offense form (14-day pay or vacate).
- Service methods: personal delivery, certified mail (return receipt requested), or first-class mail. The period runs from the date the tenant RECEIVES the notice.
