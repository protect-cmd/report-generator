INDIANA LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — EVICTION COMPLAINT PROCEEDINGS:
- Governing statutes: Ind. Code §§ 32-30-3-1 through 32-30-3-14 (recovery of real property); Ind. Code § 32-31 (landlord-tenant); Indiana Small Claims Rules
- Court: Small Claims Court for monetary claims ≤$10,000 (most residential evictions; filing fee ~$97). Circuit/Superior Court for claims >$10,000 or at landlord's choice.
- SURETY BOND REQUIRED (Ind. Code § 32-30-3-6): Indiana requires the landlord to post a surety bond when filing an eviction complaint. Amount set by court (typically double the monthly rent). Landlord pays 10–15% premium to a bonding company.
- Prerequisite: Appropriate pre-suit notice served and notice period expired. Complaint must state date and method of prior notice service.
- Service: Court issues summons served by sheriff (standard) or mail.
- Hearing: Typically 10–21 days after filing.
- Writ of Possession (Ind. Code § 32-30-3-10): Issued after judgment. Tenant gets 48 hours to 5 days to vacate voluntarily after writ is served. Sheriff enforces.
- Appeal: Tenant has 30 days from judgment to appeal to Circuit Court.
- Abandoned property (Ind. Code §§ 32-31-4-1 through 32-31-4-5): Landlord must obtain a court order before removing abandoned belongings, store them at a licensed warehouse for 90 days, and notify the tenant. Tenant may reclaim by paying storage costs within 90 days. After 90 days, warehouse may sell. CRITICAL: Landlord cannot simply discard tenant belongings.
- Self-help eviction illegal in Indiana.

GROUNDS SELECTION — based on {{reason_for_eviction}}:
- "Nonpayment": underlying notice = 10-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Vacate per Ind. Code § 32-31-1-6
- "Lease Violation": underlying notice = Notice to Cure or Quit (typically 14–30 days) per Ind. Code § 32-31-7-7; describe violation
- "End of Lease" or "No-Fault": underlying notice = 30-Day Notice to Vacate per Ind. Code § 32-31-1-1 (month-to-month), or Demand to Vacate for holdover after expired fixed-term per Ind. Code § 32-31-1-8
