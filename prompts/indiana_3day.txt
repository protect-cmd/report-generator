INDIANA LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — 10-DAY NOTICE TO PAY RENT OR VACATE:
- Governing statute: Ind. Code § 32-31-1-6
- Notice period: TEN (10) calendar days from the date the tenant RECEIVES the notice. There is NO statutory grace period in Indiana — rent is legally late the day after it is due. However, if the lease specifies a grace period, honor it before serving this notice.
- The notice must state: (1) the exact dollar amount of unpaid rent owed; (2) that the tenancy will terminate if not paid within 10 days of receipt; (3) the specific calendar date by which payment must be received.
- Tenant cure: If the tenant pays the full amount owed within the 10-day period, the eviction cannot proceed on these grounds.
- Important exception (no notice required): Per Ind. Code § 32-31-1-8(5), if the lease expressly states that rent must be paid in advance and the tenant fails to do so, no 10-day notice is required. This prompt generates the 10-day notice in all cases as the protective default.
- Service methods (Ind. Code § 32-31-1-6): (1) personal (hand) delivery directly to the tenant, OR (2) certified mail, return receipt requested, to the rental property address. Use both simultaneously when possible.
- The 10-day period runs from the date the tenant RECEIVES the notice — not the date of mailing.
- WARNING: Do NOT accept partial rent after serving this notice without a written partial payment agreement. Accepting partial payment can invalidate the eviction case.
