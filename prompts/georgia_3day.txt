GEORGIA LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — 3-DAY NOTICE TO PAY OR VACATE:
- Governing statute: O.C.G.A. § 44-7-50(c), as amended by 2024 Ga. Laws Act 392 (HB 404), effective 7/1/2024
- Notice period: THREE (3) BUSINESS DAYS. Weekends (Saturday, Sunday) and legal holidays do NOT count toward the three-day period. Count only business days when calculating the pay-or-vacate deadline.
- Amount that may be demanded: All past due rent, late fees, utilities, and other charges owed to the landlord as of the date of notice. Do NOT add amounts not reflected in the client data provided.
- Service method (O.C.G.A. § 44-7-50(d)): The notice MUST be posted in a sealed envelope conspicuously on the door of the rental property. If the rental agreement specifies additional delivery methods (e.g., email, certified mail), those additional methods must also be used. The notice is NOT required to be personally handed to the tenant — door posting in a sealed envelope is the primary statutory method.
- Required elements: Landlord name, tenant name(s), rental property address, exact total amount owed itemized by category, the three-business-day deadline, and the date of notice.
- Late fees and attorney fees: Late fees and utilities may be included in the total demand amount per the 2024 statute. Attorney fees are not required to be stated in the notice itself.
- Important 2024 law note: The three-business-day requirement applies to residential lease agreements entered into or renewed on or after July 1, 2024. For leases predating 7/1/2024 that have not been renewed, the prior law (no mandatory cure period) may apply. This service does not determine which version applies — the landlord must verify with an attorney.

--- GEORGIA SERVING INSTRUCTIONS — INCLUDE VERBATIM AT BOTTOM ---
HOW TO SERVE THIS NOTICE IN GEORGIA (O.C.G.A. § 44-7-50(d)):

Step 1: Print two (2) copies of this notice — one for the tenant and one for your records.

Step 2: Place the tenant's copy inside a sealed envelope. Do NOT leave the notice unsealed or folded and taped to the door. The statute requires a sealed envelope.

Step 3: Post the sealed envelope conspicuously on the front door of the rental property. "Conspicuously" means it must be clearly visible — eye level on the door is recommended. Do not slide it under the door or leave it in a mailbox for purposes of satisfying this requirement.

Step 4: If your lease agreement specifies additional delivery methods (for example, certified mail or email), you MUST also deliver the notice by those additional methods on the same day.

Step 5: Document your service immediately. Take a dated photograph of the envelope posted on the door. Note the date and time of posting in writing.

Step 6: Keep your copy of the notice and all documentation of service. You will need proof of service if you proceed to file a Dispossessory Affidavit.

Step 7: Count THREE (3) BUSINESS DAYS from the date of posting (excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and federal holidays). If the tenant does not pay the full amount owed or vacate by the deadline, you may proceed to file a Dispossessory Affidavit at the Magistrate Court in the county where the property is located.

IMPORTANT: Do not accept partial payment after serving this notice unless you intend to abandon the eviction. Under Georgia law, if you have already accepted a full tender and dismissed a case within the prior 12 months for the same tenant, you are not required to accept payment again. Consult an attorney before accepting any payment after this notice is served.
--- END SERVING INSTRUCTIONS ---
