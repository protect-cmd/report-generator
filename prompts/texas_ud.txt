TEXAS LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — FORCIBLE DETAINER PROCEEDINGS:
- Governing statutes: Tex. Prop. Code §§ 24.001–24.009; Texas Rules of Civil Procedure (TRCP) Rule 510
- Court: Justice of the Peace (JP) Court for the PRECINCT in which the rental property is located. Under SB 38 (eff. January 1, 2026), filing is also permitted in an adjacent precinct in the same county.
- Prerequisite: A valid written notice to vacate (per § 24.005) must have been served and the notice period expired before suit is filed.
- Petition: Sworn Petition for Forcible Detainer. Must state: property address, landlord name, tenant name(s), grounds for eviction, and that the tenant is unlawfully holding over.
- Unpaid rent: Maximum recoverable in Justice Court is $20,000 (current jurisdictional limit). Must include sworn statement per § 24.0051.
- Hearing: Scheduled no sooner than 10 days and no later than 21 days after filing (TRCP Rule 510.4(a)).
- Service: Citation served by constable or sheriff at least 4 days before hearing (TRCP Rule 510.4(b)).
- Tenant answer: Not required — tenant may appear verbally at the hearing.
- Writ of Possession: Cannot issue until at least 6 days after judgment (5-day appeal window). Expires 60 days after judgment (90 days with court approval). Tenant receives 24-hour notice before removal.
- Appeal: Tenant has 5 days from judgment to appeal to County Court for de novo trial.
- Self-help eviction is illegal: Tex. Prop. Code § 92.0081.

GROUNDS SELECTION — based on {{reason_for_eviction}}:
- "Nonpayment": underlying notice = Notice to Pay Rent or Vacate — 3-day notice per § 24.005; include amount owed
- "Lease Violation": underlying notice = Notice to Vacate — 3-day notice per § 24.005; describe violation
- "End of Lease" or "No-Fault": after § 91.001 termination is complete, underlying notice = 3-Day Notice to Vacate per § 24.005; note the two-step process
