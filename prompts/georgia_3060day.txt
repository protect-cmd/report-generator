--- GEORGIA SERVING INSTRUCTIONS — INCLUDE VERBATIM AT BOTTOM ---
HOW TO SERVE THIS NOTICE IN GEORGIA (O.C.G.A. § 44-7-7):

Step 1: Print three (3) copies of this notice — one for each method of service below, plus one for your records.

Step 2: CERTIFIED MAIL (Required for documentation): Mail one copy via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested to the tenant at the rental property address. Keep the certified mail receipt and return receipt card (green card) when returned. This is your primary proof of service.

Step 3: FIRST-CLASS MAIL: Mail a second copy via regular first-class mail to the same address on the same day. This provides a backup record.

Step 4: DOOR POSTING: Post a third copy on the front door of the rental property in a sealed envelope on the same day you mail the certified and first-class copies. Take a dated photograph of the posted notice.

Step 5: Document everything. Write down the date you mailed and posted the notice. Keep your certified mail tracking number and all receipts.

Step 6: Calculate the 60-day deadline. Count sixty (60) calendar days from the date you serve this notice (the date you mail/post it). Weekends and holidays are included in the count. The tenant must vacate by that date.

Step 7: If the tenant has not vacated by the deadline, do NOT attempt to remove the tenant yourself or change the locks. You must file a Dispossessory Affidavit at the Magistrate Court in the county where the property is located to begin the court eviction process.

IMPORTANT: If the tenant pays rent after receiving this notice, accepting that payment may constitute renewal of the tenancy and may invalidate this notice. Consult an attorney before accepting any rent payment after serving this notice.
--- END SERVING INSTRUCTIONS ---
