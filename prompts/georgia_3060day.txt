GEORGIA LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — NOTICE TO VACATE:
- Governing statute: O.C.G.A. § 44-7-7 (month-to-month / tenancy at will termination)
- Month-to-month tenancy: SIXTY (60) CALENDAR DAYS notice required. Weekends and holidays count — no business-day exclusion.
- Holdover tenant (fixed-term lease expired): No specific statutory notice period; a written demand for possession is required. Use the lease end date as the vacate deadline.
- No-fault by default: Georgia law does not require the landlord to state a reason for terminating a month-to-month tenancy.
- Service method (best practice — no specific method prescribed by § 44-7-7): Certified mail (return receipt requested) AND first-class mail AND door posting on the same day.
- The notice must state a specific calendar move-out date.
- If {{month_to_month}} is "true": 60-day notice language, move_out_date = 60 calendar days from notice date.
- If {{month_to_month}} is "false" and {{lease_end_date}} is populated: holdover/end-of-lease demand, move_out_date = {{lease_end_date}}.

OUTPUT FORMAT:
Return ONLY a valid JSON object. No markdown, no code fences, no explanation — just the raw JSON.
