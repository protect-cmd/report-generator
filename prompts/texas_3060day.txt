TEXAS LEGAL REQUIREMENTS — NOTICE TO TERMINATE TENANCY (NO-FAULT / MONTH-TO-MONTH):
- Governing statutes: Tex. Prop. Code § 91.001 (termination of tenancy) and § 24.005(a) (notice to vacate prerequisite to eviction suit)
- Texas does NOT use a fixed 30-day or 60-day statutory period. The notice period is defined by the rent-paying period:
    — Monthly tenancy (most common): Tenancy terminates on the LATER of: (1) the specific date stated in the notice, OR (2) one month (~30 days) after the date notice is given. Move-out date must be at least 30 days from date of service.

- No reason is required to terminate a month-to-month tenancy in Texas.
- IMPORTANT — Two-step process: After the tenancy terminates, if the tenant fails to vacate, the landlord must serve a SEPARATE 3-Day Notice to Vacate (per § 24.005) before filing a Forcible Detainer suit. This § 91.001 termination notice alone does not satisfy § 24.005.
