Document 4 of 4 — Magistrate Court Filing Checklist

Filing Checklist

Fulton County Magistrate Court · Eviction Filing
Before You Go to the Courthouse
Confirm you have a copy of the signed, served underlying eviction notice (Document 1)
Complete the Affidavit of Service (Document 3) and have it notarized
Have Dispossessory Affidavit (Document 2) signed in front of a notary, court clerk, or judge
Gather your lease agreement (original or copy)
Gather proof of unpaid rent or lease violation (payment history, bank records, photos, written communications)
Call the Fulton County Magistrate Court to confirm current filing fees before going
At the Courthouse
File Dispossessory Affidavit with the Magistrate Court clerk
Pay the filing fee (typically $54–$150) plus Sheriff service fee (~$35 per named defendant)
Provide copies of all documents to the clerk as required
Obtain a case number and note the date of filing
After Filing
The court will issue a Summons — the Sheriff or Marshal will serve the tenant (you do NOT serve this)
Tenant has 7 calendar days from service to file an answer
Check with the court clerk on day 8 whether an answer was filed
If No Answer Filed (Default)
File Application for Default Writ of Possession on day 8 or later
Court will schedule issuance of the Writ of Possession
If Tenant Files an Answer (Contested)
Court will schedule a hearing (typically 10–30 days out)
Bring all documentation to the hearing
Present your case to the magistrate judge
After Judgment in Your Favor
Court issues Writ of Possession (typically 7 days after judgment)
Deliver the Writ to the Fulton County Sheriff's or Marshal's office
Sheriff will execute the eviction — do NOT attempt to remove the tenant yourself
Do NOT change locks, shut off utilities, or remove tenant property before Sheriff executes the Writ
IMPORTANT: Self-help eviction (changing locks, removing property, shutting off utilities before Sheriff execution) is illegal and may result in significant civil liability. Do not attempt it.
County-Specific Filing Instructions
FULTON COUNTY MAGISTRATE COURT — SPECIFIC INSTRUCTIONS: Location: 185 Central Ave SW, Suite TG100, Atlanta, GA 30303 Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM Filing fee: Approximately $80 for the Dispossessory Affidavit plus $35 per named defendant for Sheriff service. Confirm exact fees with the clerk before arriving. Fulton County allows online filing through their e-filing portal for represented parties. Self-represented landlords should file in person at the clerk's window. After filing, the Sheriff's Civil Division will serve the Summons and Affidavit on the tenant. The tenant has 7 calendar days from service to file an answer.
